I recently heard about someone who got burned in a UPI scam, a crime that's growing more prevalent. The scam works like this: the con artist convinces the target to send money, rather than the other way around.

In this instance, the individual received a phone call from someone posing as a delivery agent. The caller claimed a refund was due and requested the person to "accept" a request via their UPI application. Without fully understanding the situation, the person entered their UPI PIN, thinking they were receiving money. However, the funds were actually taken from their account.

This kind of fraud usually targets people who are not very familiar with how digital payments work, especially students or older users. Scammers take advantage of confusion between “pay” and “collect” requests.
The consequences of these actions can be serious, leading to immediate financial loss that is often difficult to recover. This situation also causes stress and damages trust in online transactions.

This case shows how important it is to be careful while using UPI apps. One should never enter a PIN to receive money and should always verify unknown calls or requests.
