Recently, I came across a situation where a person lost money through a UPI scam, which is becoming quite common nowadays. In this type of fraud, the scammer tricks the victim into sending money instead of receiving it.

In this case, the person received a phone call from someone pretending to be a delivery agent. The caller said that a refund needed to be processed and asked the person to “accept” a request on their UPI app. Without fully understanding it, the person entered their UPI PIN, thinking they were receiving money. However, the money was actually deducted from their account.

This kind of fraud usually targets people who are not very familiar with how digital payments work, especially students or older users. Scammers take advantage of confusion between “pay” and “collect” requests.

The consequences can be serious, as money is lost instantly and is often difficult to recover. It also causes stress and loss of trust in online transactions.
This case shows how important it is to be careful while using UPI apps. One should never enter a PIN to receive money and should always verify unknown calls or requests.
